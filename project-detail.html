The Meridian House

Private Residence — São Paulo, Brazil

Residential New Build Landscape
The Project

A home in dialogue with its landscape.

The Meridian House is a private residence for a family of four, sited on a sloped lot at the edge of the Atlantic Forest in Greater São Paulo. The brief was deceptively simple: build a house that feels like it belongs here, and nowhere else.

Our response was a long, horizontal volume that follows the natural contour of the land. Concrete walls — board-formed and left raw — anchor the structure to the earth, while a cantilevered living wing opens to the forest below through full-height glass. The boundary between inside and outside dissolves.

Inside, materials shift from the cool precision of exposed concrete to the warmth of reclaimed jequitibá wood, hand-thrown ceramic tiles, and natural stone sourced within 80 km of the site. Every room is oriented to capture morning light, framing specific views of the tree canopy like living paintings.
